About Me

My name is Tima, and I have worked as a studio developer for about two to three years. I was first introduced to Blender five to six years ago, where I learned procedural techniques, sculpting with simple shapes, and the basic controls.

After my family moved to a different city, I stepped away from Blender for around a year before coming back to it. I started by making random projects until things finally clicked, and I found a job that introduced me to realistic rendering. The tasks were fairly simple at first. I created realistic planets, galaxies, and other models for websites, and this is where my interest in realism really began to grow.

Later, when I went looking for mountains, I could not find any that fit, so I started searching for a realistic terrain solver. That is when I came across Gaea and KTT for Houdini. I tried Gaea first, but I was quickly held back by its export limit. Then the developer of KTT kindly gifted the tool to me, and I began creating terrain there. Most of what you see in my renders is made with Gaea and KTT.
